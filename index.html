Chasing the Shadow — Spain Eclipse Guide 2026 & 2027
A field guide for the shadow-chaser · Schiphol → Spain

Two summers, two times the sky goes dark.

Spain sits under the Moon's shadow on 12 August 2026 and again on 2 August 2027. The 2026 path runs evening-low across the green north; 2027 climbs high over the sun-baked south. This guide picks the regions that balance a real shot at totality against heat, crowds, and a holiday worth having — one page each.

Chasing the Shadow
The evening eclipse 12 AUG 2026 · ~20:30 CEST · sun low in the WNW

An hour before sunset the shadow sweeps the green north and dry interior. Totality is short (under two minutes) and the Sun sits painfully low — so an open western horizon is non-negotiable. The trade-off below is real and unavoidable.

The catch

The cool, comfortable regions you'd want in August (the green Atlantic north) are also the cloudiest — roughly 55–60% cloud cover. The reliably clear regions (interior, islands) are either furnace-hot or packed and pricey. You cannot have cool + clear + uncrowded at once.

The relief

There's a far better eclipse next summer in the south — high sun, long totality, near-zero cloud. So treat 2026 as the relaxed one: pick the region for the holiday, take totality as a bonus, and keep your nerves for 2027.

The morning eclipse 02 AUG 2027 · ~10:47 CEST · sun high & safe

This is the easy one. Mid-morning, the Sun well clear of the horizon — no mountains or haze to dodge — totality stretching past two and a half minutes, and the famously dry Andalusian August keeping the sky cloudless. The price is heat, but the eclipse falls in the cooler morning hours.

Why it's the better eclipse

Up to ~6m 23s on the centreline (out at sea); ~2m 54s in the city of Cádiz versus barely 1m 45s anywhere in 2026. High sun means you can stand almost anywhere with a clear view. August Andalusia is about as cloud-free as Europe gets.

The catch

It's southern Spain in August — daytime highs of 30–36°C. But the eclipse is at ~10:47, before the worst heat, and the coast (Costa de la Luz) runs several degrees milder than the interior. Plan beaches and shade for the afternoons.

Eclipse times, durations and Sun altitudes are approximate and vary across each region — once you've chosen a spot, confirm the exact local circumstances and check for ridgelines or buildings on your western horizon (critical in 2026, when the Sun is only a few degrees up).
